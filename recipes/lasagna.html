Lasagna

About this recipe

Imagine a rich tapestry of flavors woven together in layers of tender pasta sheets, savory meat sauce, and velvety cheese, creating a timeless masterpiece known as lasagna. This cherished recipe holds a special place in the heart of a family friend, its origins tracing back to his great-grandmother who journeyed to the United States in the aftermath of World War I. With each succulent bite, one can taste the heritage and tradition that has been lovingly preserved through generations.

Rooted in the culinary traditions of Italy, this lasagna recipe bears the unique imprint of its immigrant matriarch, blending robust Italian spices with the fresh ingredients found in the new world. As the aroma of simmering tomatoes, garlic, and herbs fills the kitchen, memories of bustling family gatherings and laughter echo through time. Each layer of pasta delicately embraces the hearty meat sauce, while creamy ricotta and mozzarella cheeses meld together in a symphony of flavors. Baked to golden perfection, this lasagna is more than just a dish; it's a testament to resilience, love, and the enduring legacy of a journey across oceans and generations.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) of tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 15 ounces of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the ground beef or Italian sausage to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spoon, and cook until browned.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1 1/2 cups of mozzarella cheese, Parmesan cheese, and egg. Mix well.
  6. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  7. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top, slightly overlapping.
  8. Spread half of the cheese mixture evenly over the noodles, then top with a layer of meat sauce. Repeat with another layer of noodles, cheese mixture, and meat sauce.
  9. Finish with a final layer of noodles, meat sauce, and remaining mozzarella cheese.
  10. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  11. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before slicing. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley before serving. Enjoy your homemade lasagna, filled with the warmth and history of generations past!
