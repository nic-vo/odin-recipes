Chicken Tagine

About this recipe

Chicken tagine is a dish deeply rooted in Moroccan culinary heritage, with a history dating back centuries. The tagine itself refers to both the cooking vessel and the flavorful stew prepared within it. Traditionally, tagines were cooked over coals or low flames, allowing the ingredients to simmer slowly and develop rich, complex flavors. The dish likely originated in North Africa, where it served as a practical solution for cooking meats and vegetables in a region with limited water resources.

Over time, chicken tagine evolved to incorporate a diverse array of ingredients, reflecting the cultural sway of the Mediterranean, Middle East, and beyond. The dish typically features tender pieces of chicken, seasoned with aromatic spices such as saffron, cumin, and cinnamon. Alongside the chicken, a colorful medley of vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and olives adds both texture and depth to the stew. With its fragrant aroma and comforting taste, chicken tagine remains a beloved staple in Moroccan cuisine, cherished for its ability to bring people together around the communal dining table.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Pinch of saffron threads
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup green olives, pitted
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a tagine or large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pan, and saut√© until softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  3. Season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper, then add them to the pan. Cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per side.
  4. Stir in the ground cumin, ginger, cinnamon, turmeric, and saffron threads, coating the chicken evenly with the spices.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth and diced tomatoes, stirring to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  6. Reduce the heat to low, cover the tagine or skillet, and let the chicken simmer gently for about 30 minutes, or until cooked through and tender.
  7. Add the green olives to the tagine, stirring them into the sauce.
  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  9. Garnish the chicken tagine with chopped cilantro and parsley before serving.
  10. Serve the chicken tagine hot, accompanied by couscous or crusty bread for a complete meal.
