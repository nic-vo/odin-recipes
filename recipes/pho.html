Pho

About this recipe

Pho, a beloved Vietnamese noodle soup, boasts a rich history deeply intertwined with Vietnam's cultural heritage. Its origins can be traced back to the early 20th century, emerging in the northern regions of Vietnam. Influenced by French colonialism, pho's evolution began with the introduction of beef into Vietnamese cuisine. The dish gradually gained popularity as street vendors and local eateries perfected their own unique variations, blending indigenous flavors with foreign influences. Initially served as a breakfast staple, pho soon became a beloved national dish, symbolizing Vietnam's culinary prowess and resilience in the face of adversity.

In modern times, pho has transcended its traditional roots to become a global culinary sensation, inspiring chefs around the world to put their own innovative twists on the classic dish. From vegan and vegetarian renditions to fusion interpretations that incorporate diverse ingredients and culinary techniques, modern takes on pho showcase its versatility and adaptability. In cosmopolitan cities like Los Angeles, Melbourne, and London, food enthusiasts can indulge in creative interpretations of pho, featuring unconventional toppings, artisanal broths, and inventive presentations. Despite these contemporary adaptations, the essence of pho—its comforting broth, delicate rice noodles, and fragrant herbs—remains at the heart of every bowl, serving as a testament to the enduring legacy of this beloved Vietnamese comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 8 cups beef broth
  • 1 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 3 star anise
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (such as cilantro, Thai basil, and mint) for garnish
  • Bean sprouts, lime wedges, and sliced chili for serving

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Pour in the beef broth and add the cinnamon sticks, star anise, and cloves. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
  3. While the broth is simmering, cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and set aside.
  4. Strain the broth to remove the aromatics and return it to the pot. Stir in the fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Add the sliced beef to the broth and let it cook for 1-2 minutes until just cooked through.
  6. To serve, divide the cooked noodles among serving bowls. Ladle the hot broth and beef over the noodles.
  7. Garnish each bowl with fresh herbs and serve with bean sprouts, lime wedges, and sliced chili on the side for customization.
  8. Enjoy your homemade pho!
